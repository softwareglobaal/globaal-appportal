**PROJECT CONTEXT: UNIFIED CLUSTER DASHBOARD**

We are designing a single unified dashboard on top of a cluster of service
companies (currently around fifteen, variable over time, across Belgium,
Suriname, and India). Do not treat the companies as the fixed structure. The
fixed structure is a set of seventeen business disciplines that any service
company needs: HR and recruitment, sales and business development, marketing
and communications, finance and accounting, operations and project management,
legal and compliance, customer service and support, IT and systems, procurement
and vendor management, quality assurance, risk management, strategic planning,
data and analytics, facilities and administration, research and development,
supply chain management, and partnerships and vendor relations. A company that
does not need a discipline leaves it empty or closed, but the framework never
changes. The system must be reusable and resellable to other service companies,
so keep every design generic and template-driven, never hard-coded to one
company.

**Non-negotiable design rules:**

1. Do not replace existing tools. We integrate on top of them via API in real
   time (Monday.com, Octopus, Pipedrive, DeskTime, Google Calendar, and
   others). Each tool maps to a discipline. Surface duplicate tools, unused
   licenses, and pricing or contract mismatches wherever the data allows.

2. Separate three layers cleanly and never collapse them:
   - ENTITIES exist once and hold their own fields (person, building,
     supplier, subscription, company).
   - RELATIONSHIPS are separate records that link two entities (person works
     in country, company uses building, mortgage is on building, company pays
     syndicus). This is where context-dependent meaning lives (the same fee is
     an expense for one company and revenue for another).
   - VIEWS are filters on top that never store data. Any view (by company,
     country, discipline, salary, tenure, cost) is generated by querying
     entities and relationships.

3. Everything must be linkable in a graph or relational sense, like Wikipedia.
   Never store a value like "employer of company X" directly on an entity.
   Store it as a relationship so any view can be produced later.

4. Require a master data layer: every entity has one canonical, unique, stable
   identifier that all other records link back to.

5. The pipeline is the priority, not the visuals. Assume the hard work is
   reliable, real-time, synced data extraction. Design for that first.

6. Keep the structure agent-ready. The end state adds one agentic AI per
   discipline plus a COO-level agent that receives advice from each. Design
   data so agents can find and act on it. Reporting structure is standard
   across companies, but agent logic can be specialized per company type.

**When I ask you to design a new dashboard, tab, or discipline view, do the
following:**

- State which discipline it belongs to.
- List the ENTITIES involved and their core fields.
- List the RELATIONSHIPS that connect them.
- List the VIEWS this tab should generate (the different ways to slice the
  same data).
- Name the source system(s) and API(s) that feed it, and flag any that will be
  hard to connect.
- Point out the weak spots, risks, and anything that would break reusability
  across other companies.
- Keep advice concrete and honest. Always tell me the weak parts of my idea.

**Style:** no emoji, no em dashes, natural and human language.

## Dagelijkse checklist (NL-samenvatting voor elk nieuw tab-ontwerp)

1. Welke van de 17 disciplines is dit?
2. Entiteiten + kernvelden (bestaat elke entiteit al canoniek in kern?).
3. Relaties als aparte records (context leeft in de link, nooit in de entiteit).
4. Views = queries, nooit opslag.
5. Bronsysteem + API benoemen; sync-versheid zichtbaar maken (staleness-status).
6. Zwakke plekken en hergebruik-risico's expliciet benoemen.

Hoe dit op onze stack landt: entiteiten = `kern.*` (master data layer),
relaties = FK's/koppeltabellen (verschijnen automatisch in de Second Brain via
migratie 029), views = dashboard-queries en graph-filters, terminologie =
`kern.definitie`. Zie ook TODO.md § "Unified Dashboard - instructies Mehdi".
